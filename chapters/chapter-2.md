# Chapter 2

Autumn and Brianna were standing in front of Shrikantha body. A breeze of remorse flew through Brianna but Autumn was stern. Both Autumn and Brianna knew everything about Shrikantha. His family has left him alone in the storm and he was a hurting loner since then. Autumn to some extent had developed some sort of hatred of the traditions against those traditions which just hurt Shrikantha.  Autumn was a compassionate woman. She knew, her family and Shrinkantha are only people she could trust. Her parents died years back and now Shrikantha had also just gone.

Shrikantha had a habit of writing down something every night, he called samikaran. His love for mathematics had just gone far. But he never showed Autumn what it was. It was a cloud document, which was designed in such a way that if Shrikanth doesn't access it for two days, its link will reach to Autumn. Shrikantha always wanted Autumn to have kindness and he knew that last lesson has to be done without him. 

Autumn knew that Shrinkantha wanted that his body be burnt and remains shall be flown into river. She decided to complete the process quickly. She called Ian, town's Mayor and expressed her wish. Ian understood the scenario and he respected both Shrikantha and Autumn. Moreover, Autumn was the richest person and after Shrikantha's death, she was going to be sole heir too. Ian knew that he is Mayor only because of her. Autumn was a compassionate and yet fierce. Shrikantha had suggested Ian name for Mayor to Autumn years back. Ian was running a construction company with work expanded to other towns as well. Ian is a simple man and Autumn knew that. 

Whole town respected Shrikantha and Autumn. By this time, everybody, kids, adults, men women all had surrounded the hospital. It was its beauty, that Sheriff Robert has just registered the case but hadn't summoned against Brianna and allowed people. Town had its own vibe and both Shrikantha and Autumn were responsible for it. Shrikantha had given an idea and Autumn had opened a vocational skill center as well on the outskirts of town, where boys and girls could play and learn after school hours and parents themselves were now running it.

Next day, Shrikantha was burnt at the town hall and his remains were flown into the river, running by the town. One story was down and another was to unfold.
